Research Projects

Encryption based on physically unclonable function generators

Prof Nirat Ray, IIT Delhi | (Jan,2024-Present)


Working on the fabrication of Physically Unclonable Functions for secured communications immune to ML modelling attacks. I am working on chip design to exploit conductivity and memristor effects of gold quantum dots to achieve high encoding capacity.

Tunable Plasmonic Lenses for sub-wavelength Optical Microscopy

Prof B. Krishna, IIT Delhi | (May,2023-Oct,2023)


Worked on the simulation of Tunable Plasmonic Lenses for sub-Wavelength Optical Microscopy. As a part of this project I simulated Light-Matter interaction for various sub-wavelength apertures to achieve the desired electric field confinement and enhancement effects. Worked on tunability of lens for THz frequency range using temperature and external voltage variation. Was awarded Undergraduate Research Award for successful completion of project.

Improving conductivity and mechanical stability of Lithium batteries using LLCZN nanoscale additives

Prof Seeram Ramakrishna, NUS | (May,2024-August,2024)


Working on LLCZN Compounds as nanoscale additives in Polymer based solid electrolytes for Lithium batteries.My focus is to decrease size of additives following a top-down synthesis approach and increasing ionic conductivity of electrolytes without compromising flexibility and mechanical strength.
